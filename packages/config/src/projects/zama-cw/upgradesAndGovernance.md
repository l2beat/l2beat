Zama Confidential Tokens governance is implemented through an Aragon 'DAO' deployment controlled by two multisigs: ZamaGovMultisigA with a {{multisigAStats}} threshold and ZamaGovMultisigB with a {{multisigBStats}} threshold. The Gateway contracts are controlled separately by the SafeL2 multisig listed in the permissions section. These accounts can upgrade contracts or change critical configuration without a timelock.

Confidential token owners and underlying token owners can freeze users. The Ethereum ACL owner can manage ACL-level account blocking, unpause the ACL, change HCU limits, and create KMS contexts with arbitrary signer sets and thresholds. A new context immediately becomes the default verifier authority and can attest unwrap amounts for assets deposited before it existed. Superseded contexts remain equally authoritative for all past and future ciphertext handles until the ACL owner explicitly destroys them; the current context cannot be destroyed.

Offchain components like the coprocessor and KMS are trusted for liveness, privacy and security and are currently not decentralized.
